My name is **Luke**, I'm a software engineer from **Phoenix, Arizona**, and I've been working in the industry for about 7 years now (at the time of this post).

I love software because I still idealistically (and maybe naively) believe that technology can make our lives better in almost every way. It is precisely this held belief that has driven me this far into my career. 

If I'm not coding, you can find me writing and producing music, collecting and building Legos (I'm a nerd I know), or tinkering with my house and improving my home decor/work environment. I also love to travel and try out new restaurants with my beautiful fiancé.

Fitness and health is very important to me. Getting in shape was by far one of the best decisions I ever made for my life, and although I don't always practice what I preach, I try to hit the gym at least 3 times a week, and get outside a lot by walking my dog Astro. 

My personality type is [INFJ](https://www.verywellmind.com/infj-introverted-intuitive-feeling-judging-2795978), if that's your sort of thing. We make up just 1% of the male population. This makes me idealistic, sensitive, passionate, reserved, and a bit of a hopeless creative and artist. I am also very focused on the future, and value deep, personal relationships.

I think everything in life comes down to human experience. After all, life is ultimately just a long, continuous sequence of experiences, all strung-together in perfect order - with _YOU_ in the driver's seat as the primary user.

I believe that you can design your life in such a way that it is deeply enjoyable, fulfilling, and full of growth and opportunity. This is why self-improvement is my primary focus, as I believe it is the foundation of success in any domain. It just feels _good_ to be growing and becoming more than you are, I think that's what life is all about.

If you made it this far, thanks for listening. I'm not much of a writer, but I'm trying to make a more concious effort towards improving my communication skills and this feels like a good way to do so. Until next time – take care!
